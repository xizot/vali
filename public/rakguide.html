Always shave with hair

Always shave in the same direction as your hair grows. Get to know the beard by feeling and analyzing how it grows on the face and neck. This can help you prevent irritation, flatulence and ingrown hairs. The pictures above show typical directions, but your beard growth can grow in other directions, it is important that you feel first. You may want to try shaving in other directions after shaving the hair once. However, the risk of irritation and ingrown hair increases if you shave off mumps.

Preparation

The best and easiest way to prepare for shaving is a shower. The warm water and steam open up your pores and soften the hair. Another effective way to prepare for shaving is to keep a moist warm towel against the skin for at least one minute. Immediately afterwards, you can massage the shaving oil onto the skin to get an extra layer of protection and improve the slide on the razor blade.

Lather

Now it's time to create your shaving cream. After preparation, it is important to spend time building up good lather with the help of shaving cream and shaving brush. We recommend using a shaving brush as it lifts the hair, softens and exfoliates the skin by removing dead skin cells. The result is a softer, closer and irritation-free shave.

  1. 01

    Rinse your shaving brush with warm water and then shake off excess water from the shaving brush. The moist shaving brush will help you create a nice and thick lather.

  2. 02

    Spread some shaving cream on the skin (1-2st clicks, about almond size) or put on the shaving brush. Then continue working on the skin with circular movements. Pour a few extra drops of water on the shaving brush if the lather is too hard. Begin shaving when you feel that the lather is fluffy and soft enough.

  3. 03

    Set aside shaving brush and shaving cream. Ensure that the shaving cream is properly sealed after use to ensure consistency and durability.

Technique

  1. 01

    Place the razor straight at a 90 degree angle to the skin and then begin to angle down until the blade hits the skin. This is probably for most people at a 30-40 degree angle (see image above).

  2. 02

    Work with light hand. Use short haircuts and straight hair along the direction of the hair.

  3. 03

    Do not press the skin but let the weight of the planer do the job.

When you are done, rinse with lukewarm water first and feel if you are satisfied with the shave. If not, run a "second pass" and reread again and run one last fine tune. When done, rinse the planer in warm water, wipe it off to maintain shine and durability. Also, do not forget the shaving brush that needs to be washed and left to dry in a moisture-free place.

Termination

When you are completely satisfied with your shaving, rinse with cold water to close the pores and gently pat dry with a towel. Then moisturize the skin with our aftershave balm.

Tips & Tricks
